Card philosophy
One card per person — you own it
Everyone gets a cardNo budget scopingYou keep your own receiptsAuto-lock at 7 days late

The ideas behind the app — how money is organized, who's responsible for what, and why it's built this way. Written for humans, not accountants.
Two levels · Central & Chapters

Public Worship is one organization made of local chapters. Each chapter runs its own events and holds its own money — and everything rolls up so leadership can see the whole picture.

Central finance manager
Sees every chapter rolled up — by shared template and by chapter — plus the global total. Sets org-wide policy. Doesn't run any one chapter's day-to-day.
rolls up from
Boston chapter manager
Runs Boston's budgets, approves its reimbursements, issues its cards. Scoped to Boston.
New York chapter manager
The same, for New York — and can't see Boston's detail.
Each chapter's money sits in its own bank account. Because chapters run the same templates (Worship with Strangers…), Central can ask "what does this event cost across all of us?"
Where every dollar lives
per instanceProjects & events
Money you budget each time you do something. "Every Worship with Strangers gets $500", split into Food / Production / Supplies. A one-off (Newcomer Dinner) is the same idea without the repeat.
recurringRecurring buckets
Standing costs on a clock — Operations monthly (software, storage, facilities), Equipment yearly, Team fun quarterly. Set the amount and cadence once.
One flexible model. Whether it's "$500 per event" or "$4,000 a year", it's the same idea underneath — a pot of money, a cadence, and categories. New budget shapes are config, not new code.
Planned vs. spent · never counted twice
Two numbers that never mix. Estimated is what you plan to spend (a shopping list, a vendor quote). Actual is money that truly left the account. The dashboard shows them side by side with the budget — so a plan is never mistaken for a real expense, and paying someone back isn't counted as buying the thing a second time.
Cards · everyone's responsible
One card per person
Every team member gets their own card — not tied to a budget. The point: for every charge, one specific person is on the hook for the receipt and the coding. Cards auto-lock if a receipt is more than 7 days late, and unlock the second it's added.
Oops, that was personal
Used the church card for a personal coffee by accident? Flag it and pay it back in one tap — from your own debit card or bank. It posts as a credit, so the books stay clean and nobody has to chase you.
Receipts, reconciliation & AI
Every charge needs a receipt and a home (which event/project + category). To make that painless, AI proposes the coding the moment a charge lands — from the card, the time, the merchant, and what's on the calendar that week. A human confirms; the AI never moves money on its own.
Reimbursements · for anyone without a card
Volunteers and guests don't carry cards. They use a public link to submit what they spent, with receipts, tied to an event. A chapter manager approves (unplanned or large spends should get approval first), and the app pays them by direct deposit. Same budgets, same coding — just money going out instead of a card charge.

Money architecture

The real money lives in Increase — one bank Account per chapter. That account is the source of truth: budgets are logical allocations of its balance, member cards are issued on it, and ACH reimbursements pay out from it. Stripe Financial Connections is read-only, only to pull in existing/legacy external bank & card spend during the transition.

Chapter OS — Expo app + web (one codebase)
The Finances screens you just clicked through. React Native + NativeWind, served native & on the web at events.publicworship.life.
Convex queries / mutations / actions
Convex backend — finances · reimbursements · payouts · increase · stripeFinance
Funds, budgets, transactions, approvals, the finance-role gate, and the two webhook handlers. All chapter-scoped.
Increase — Account per chapter
Holds the balance · issues cards (real-time auth) · originates ACH payouts. One Entity + Account per chapter via the Platform API. Webhook: /increase/webhook.
Stripe Financial Connections
Read-only sync of existing external bank + credit-card accounts (Increase can't aggregate outside accounts). Reuses the existing /stripe/webhook.

All money is integer amountCents (matches ticketing, never floats). Every table carries chapterId + a by_chapter index. Ownership is personId → people, the house convention. A budgets row carries a scope (project · event template · a specific instance · or a standing bucket) and a cadence — so "WwS = $500 per instance" and "Equipment = $4,000/yr" are the same table.
Budget shapes & AI coding one model, many cadences
A budget can be scoped & timed any way
per event instancemonthlyquarterlyyearlyone-off
Allocate "$500 each time we run Worship with Strangers", "$650/mo Operations", "$4,000/yr Equipment", "$600/qtr Team fun" — all the same budgets table. New budget types are config, not code.
AI coding of transactions
A Convex action (an LLM) proposes fund + category + event/instance link for each incoming charge from the card, time, merchant & the week's calendar. A human accepts — the model never posts money on its own. Feeds the reconcile queue.
Central roll-up templates across chapters
Because chapters run the same templates (Worship with Strangers, Love Thy Neighbor…), Central sums each template's spend across every chapter — plus per-chapter and global totals — while each chapter still drills into its exact instances. A central finance manager reads across all chapters; a chapter manager is scoped to theirs (a new tier above the chapter finance role).
The anti-double-counting rule
Estimated (planned)
Open supplies fields.cost, vendor commitments, project/event budget caps. What we expect to spend.
Actual (spent)
The transactions table only — card spend, synced legacy spend, reimbursement payouts. What actually moved.
The dashboard shows Estimated / Actual / Budget as three separate numbers and never adds an estimate to an actual. Reimbursement payouts post as flow: "transfer" so paying a person back isn't counted as buying the thing twice.
Authorization new finance role gate
viewer — read dashboardsbookkeeper — categorize & reconcilemanager — budgets, approve, pay, issue cards
A graded financeRoles table (mirrors lib/superuser.ts). Team members are none of these — they see only their own card & requests. Separation of duties: a manager can never approve their own reimbursement; large payouts can require a second approver.
